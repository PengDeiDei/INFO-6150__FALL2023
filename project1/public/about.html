My past:

Me and my siblings were first found stray near the city of Zillah. We were rescued and sent to Yakima Humane for care and treatment. Due to the lack of available space, we were transferred to Seattle Humane two months later. However, during the long transportation without enough caring, we were unfortunately affected by FeLV while we were weak and slim with merely 2 lbs. Fortunately, as we arrived at Seattle Huame, we were immediately adopted by several kind families.

Me after being adopted:

After being adopted from Seattle Humane, my family fed me as much and diverse as they could. I could receive 5 meals a day from 6 in the morning to 12 at night, including goat milk, egg yolk, cat canned food, dry cat food, and other supplements.Besides, my parents were keen on inviting friends to visit me and take me to explore the outdoors.Though I still don't get along too easily with other cats, I've grown into a brave cat who is close to people.

Breif about the shelter I from:

Seattle Humane is a renowned animal welfare organization dedicated to providing compassionate care, rescue, and support to animals in need in the greater Seattle area. Founded in 1897, this non-profit organization has a rich history of promoting the well-being of animals and their relationships with humans. Seattle Humane is committed to the mission of saving and serving pets in the community, offering a wide range of programs and services, including animal adoptions, veterinary care, education, and advocacy. With a passionate team of staff and volunteers, Seattle Humane strives to create a world where every animal can experience love, care, and a forever home.
